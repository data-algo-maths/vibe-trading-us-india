Perform an evidence-bound, paper-only analysis of RELIANCE.NS for the Indian stock market.

MARKET AND DATA RULES
- Treat RELIANCE.NS as an Indian equity only.
- Use the repository's market-data, backtesting, technical-analysis and risk tools.
- Use only approved India sources under the india_strict policy.
- Do not switch to Tushare, AKShare, Eastmoney, crypto, US, Hong Kong, China or another market.
- Benchmark the stock against ^NSEI for NSE stocks or ^BSESN for BSE stocks.
- State the actual data provider, first observation date, latest observation date and latest closing price.
- Verify that the latest observation is reasonably current for the latest completed Indian trading session.
- If required price, benchmark, fundamental or financial-statement data is missing, stale, invalid or unavailable, mark the affected analysis VOIDED.
- Never invent or estimate missing data.

ANALYSIS HORIZONS
Analyze the stock separately for:

1. Long term: 1 to 5 years
2. Swing: approximately one month, reviewed weekly
3. Tactical: daily end-of-day paper signals

FUNDAMENTAL ANALYSIS
When reliable data is available, evaluate:

- revenue, earnings and operating-profit growth
- operating and net margins
- return on equity and return on capital
- operating cash flow and free cash flow
- debt, interest coverage and liquidity
- promoter ownership and pledge, when applicable
- valuation using P/E, P/B, EV/EBITDA and free-cash-flow yield
- valuation relative to its historical range and Indian sector peers
- recent official NSE/BSE filings and material corporate events
- business quality, competitive position, governance and key risks

Clearly separate verified facts, calculated metrics and assumptions. If fundamentals cannot be verified, mark the fundamental section VOIDED without voiding valid price-only analysis.

TECHNICAL ANALYSIS
Calculate and interpret:

- 20, 50, 100 and 200-session moving averages
- RSI
- MACD
- ATR and recent volatility
- volume trend
- support and resistance
- trend strength
- breakout and mean-reversion conditions
- maximum drawdown
- correlation and beta against the selected Indian benchmark

BACKTESTING
Use the repository backtesting tools to test, when sufficient history exists:

- moving-average trend
- dual momentum
- Donchian breakout
- z-score mean reversion
- buy-and-hold benchmark

Use next-session execution to prevent look-ahead bias. Include realistic Indian delivery transaction costs, slippage and turnover. Use walk-forward or chronological out-of-sample validation. Do not optimize and evaluate parameters on the same period.

Report:

- total and annualized return
- benchmark return and excess return
- Sharpe ratio
- maximum drawdown
- volatility
- turnover
- number of trades
- win rate, when available
- exposure
- transaction-cost impact
- in-sample versus out-of-sample performance
- data source and tested date range

RISK ANALYSIS
Assess:

- company-specific and sector risks
- valuation risk
- liquidity and gap risk
- volatility and drawdown risk
- concentration risk
- event and governance risk
- strategy failure conditions
- data quality limitations

FINAL OUTPUT
Provide:

1. Data-integrity status: VALID or VOIDED
2. Executive summary
3. Fundamental assessment
4. Valuation assessment
5. Technical assessment
6. Backtest comparison table
7. Long-term 1-5 year research view
8. One-month swing research view
9. Daily tactical paper signal
10. Risks and invalidation conditions
11. Data limitations
12. Paper-trade journal entry

For each horizon, classify the result as:

- RESEARCH CANDIDATE
- WATCHLIST
- AVOID
